The primary constituent of cinnamon bark oil is (E)-cinnamaldehyde. In 'True' Ceylon cinnamon, this makes up 65–85% of the volatile oil. #### 🍃 2. Eugenol (The Leaf Powerhouse)
While the bark is rich in aldehydes, the leaves of the cinnamon tree are dominated by Eugenol (often exceeding 70%). This is the same compound found in cloves. #### 🪵 3. Camphor (The Root Secret)
If you were to distill the roots of a cinnamon tree, you wouldn't get the smell of spice at all—you would get Camphor. The root-bark oil is distinctively cooling and medicinal, showing how the tree partitions its chemical defenses throughout its structure.
